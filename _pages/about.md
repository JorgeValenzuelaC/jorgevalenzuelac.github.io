I am a PhD student in the **Doctorado en Ingeniería de Sistemas Complejos** (Complex Systems Engineering) program at **Universidad Adolfo Ibáñez**, in Santiago, Chile. I hold a degree in Civil Mathematical Engineering (Ingeniería Civil Matemática) and a Master's in Applied Mathematics from Universidad de Chile.

My research interests sit at the intersection of quantum computing, graph theory, and computational complexity, with a particular focus on the **design and analysis of distributed quantum algorithms**: what quantum resources can and cannot do when computation is spread across multiple parties with limited communication.

Recently, my work has centered on communication complexity in the multiparty Simultaneous Message Passing (SMP) model, and on locally checkable proofs and certification in distributed networks under the LOCAL model.

Research visits
------
Over the second half of 2025 I visited several groups working on distributed and quantum computing:

* **Aalto University**, Helsinki, Finland — distributed computing in the LOCAL model (November 2025)
* **IMDEA Networks**, Madrid, Spain — unambiguous quantum discrimination (October 2025)
* **LIRIS, CNRS**, Lyon, France — locally checkable proof (LCP) schemes on distributed networks (October 2025)

Teaching

Alongside research, I have taught at Universidad de Chile as a teaching assistant for *Cálculo Avanzado y Aplicaciones* (Spring 2024), and as an instructor (Profesor Auxiliar) for *Calculabilidad y Complejidad Computacional* (Fall 2026).
