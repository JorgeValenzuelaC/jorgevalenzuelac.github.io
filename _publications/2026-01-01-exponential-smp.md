The Simultaneous Message Passing (SMP) model provides a fundamental framework for studying the relative power of classical and quantum communication in theoretical computer science. In the two-player setting, it is well known that shared randomness (the public-coin model) and quantum communication are incomparable.

In this work, we establish an exponential separation between the public-coin and quantum SMP models for a natural *k*-party generalization of Index Coordination, which we denote IC<sub>k,n</sub>. We show that a public-coin protocol can solve the *k*-party relation IC<sub>k,n</sub> using *O(k log n)* bits, whereas any unambiguous quantum protocol without shared entanglement requires Ω((1/k) n<sup>1-1/k</sup>) qubits. This yields an exponential separation for constant *k = O(1)*, and a polynomial separation for *k = Θ(log n)*.

Joint work with Pedro Montealegre and Iván Rapaport.
